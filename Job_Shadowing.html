The LYFeX Job Shadowing Program is a transformative learning experience based on the principle of observation-designed to help students explore various career paths, develop cognitive skills, and gain clarity about their future. Through real-world exposure, students observe professionals, learn from their expertise, and discover their true interests in a supportive environment- It's completely self-learning.
How LYFeX Shadowing Works

Understanding Interests

LYFeX team meets with students to understand their interests, strengths, and aspirations through one-on-one or group discussions.

Expert Matchmaking

Based on the insights, students are paired with professionals (mentors) in relevant fields for the shadowing program.

⁠Orientation Program
(2 Days)

To prepare students for the shadowing experience by explaining expectations and behavior standards.Explanation of the shadowing process—observing, learning, and self-driven.

Shadowing Program
(3 Months)

Observation-Based Learning: Students spend time with mentors, observing daily activities, decision-making, and workflows in real-world settings.

⁠Self-Learning Focus

Mentors won’t explicitly teach; instead, students develop cognitive skills by analyzing and reflecting on what they observe.Students are encouraged to ask questions and seek clarification to deepen their understanding.

Post- Program Reflection

At the end of the 3 months, students reflect on whether the field and mentor align with their interests.
If yes, the program continues for another quarter.
If no, students can explore a different field or mentor in the next cycle.

Continuation

Students can continue the shadowing program until they find their passion and an ideal mentor.

Reporting

LYFeX provides a detailed report to students, parents, and schools, outlining the student’s progress and observations.

Behavioral Analysis

No grades are assigned. Instead, a behavioral analysis report is included, focusing on:
Cognitive development
Engagement and initiative
Problem-solving and adaptability

Certificate

A yearly certificate is awarded, summarizing the student’s participation and achievements.
